## Summary

Improve XVF3800 data collection by aligning backend/frontend channel and DOA conventions, exporting raw-channel diagnostics, and fixing raw audio capture so saved WAVs are buffered before pipeline drops.

## What Changed

- defaulted live XVF3800 capture to device channels `2..5` and aligned them with mic `1..4`
- aligned XVF3800 DOA so `0°` is at the cable edge and removed the frontend-only rotation compensation
- added per-recording scene annotations in Data Collection and exported them into `metadata.json`
- taught `beamforming.benchmark.data_collection_benchmark` to use recording `metadata.json` as ground truth when present
- exported raw-channel plots and raw per-channel WAV playback for Data Collection recordings
- fixed live raw audio export to buffer directly from the input callback and use the actual stream sample rate, avoiding corrupted/static WAVs when the processing queue drops frames

## Why

This makes real XVF3800 recordings usable for algorithm tuning, keeps channel/DOA conventions consistent across UI and backend, and prevents raw capture artifacts caused by saving audio too late in the live pipeline.
